## Key Takeaways

1. **OpenMM is ~150× faster** than Tinker per evaluation (~190 eval/s vs
   ~1.3 eval/s).  OpenMM runs in-process; Tinker spawns a subprocess per call.

2. **Nelder-Mead** is the most reliable derivative-free optimizer, reaching
   near-zero scores for small molecules.  **L-BFGS-B** with finite-difference
   gradients is faster per iteration but may not fully converge on shallow
   objective landscapes.

3. **L-BFGS-B diverges on high-dimensional frequency objectives.**  With
   182 parameters and 9 molecules, finite-difference gradients are unstable
   — the optimizer *worsened* the score.  Nelder-Mead is the reliable choice
   for derivative-free frequency optimization on complex systems.

4. **The Seminario method is effectively free** — even 182-parameter
   organometallic systems complete in < 50 ms.

5. **Scaling**: With Nelder-Mead and 9 molecules (1,030 frequency
   references), the full optimization converges in ~370 s (~6 min), achieving
   76.7% score improvement.  Each evaluation computes frequencies for all
   training molecules.
